DESCRIPTION: You are invited to participate in a research study on understanding human-chatbot communication and evaluating the performance of chatbots on certain tasks. Our goal is to learn how to build chatbots that can talk to people naturally, much like another human being does. You will chat with a chatbot for around 10-15 minutes. The chatbot is designed to discuss a range of social topics (for example music or travel) and you are encouraged to converse with it naturally, as you would with a friend. For example, you may ask questions or introduce your own topics. Once you have completed your conversation, you will be asked to answer feedback questions about the interaction. Data from responses and chatlogs will be analysed in deidentified format and extracts edited to preserve confidentiality may be featured in any published work resulting out of the study.

TIME INVOLVEMENT: Your participation will take between 10-15 minutes.

RISKS AND BENEFITS: There are no foreseeable risks to participating in this study. During your participation, you may experience a lack of interest in sustaining the conversation and may receive replies that are not coherent or easy to understand. Some of the responses you receive may be repetitive, and you may be asked to discuss topics that are not interesting to you. To help reduce risks, the research team has built features that will detect complaints. If you are no longer enjoying the conversation, you will be free to end it at any time, by writing “stop.” The research team expects to learn about perceptions of chatbots from this project, which we hope will improve the quality of future chatbots. You may receive any public benefit that may come from these research results being shared with the greater scientific community. We cannot and do not guarantee or promise that you will receive any benefits from your participation in this study.

PAYMENTS: You will receive $3 as payment for your participation.

PARTICIPANT’S RIGHTS: If you have read this form and have decided to participate in this project, please understand your participation is voluntary and you have the right to withdraw your consent or discontinue participation at any time without penalty or loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled. The alternative is not to participate. You have the right to refuse to answer particular questions. The results of this research study may be presented at scientific or professional meetings or published in scientific journals. Your individual privacy will be maintained in all published and written data resulting from the study.
